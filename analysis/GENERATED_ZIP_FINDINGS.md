# Generated-ZIP findings

## Scoped conclusion

Generated-ZIP downstream reuse failure was repeatedly observed for one account from 10–16 July 2026 through the recorded routes.

The published test stratification is intentionally explicit:

- 2/2 recorded direct picker comparisons failed while uploaded controls worked.
- 9/9 recorded assistant-side exact-target audits did not resolve an independently usable generated ZIP.
- No success was identified among the combined eleven recorded targets.

The direct comparisons and assistant-side audits are not the same experimental surface. They are presented together only as a scoped account-history pattern, not as a homogeneous controlled sample.

## Controlled specimen

The published 133-byte ZIP is valid and has one 11-byte payload. Its present hash equals the prior recorded uploaded operational-copy hash. This removes malformed current bytes, payload difference, archive size, and archive complexity as simple explanations for the recorded route difference.

It does not identify the internal cause. Plausible unresolved classes include enrollment, object state, index coverage, picker eligibility, attachment, materialisation, naming, suffix, and lineage. The evidence does not select among them.

## Cohort limits

The 31-row inventory is a retrospective first-party cohort with sequential release families. It is not 31 independent accounts or test conditions. Only part of its source-pointer set resolves in the private archive, and one of the eleven test filenames does not exactly join the inventory. “No success identified” must not become a measured 0/31 rate.

## Operational result

Rename-and-reupload created an operational replacement object in the recorded specimens. That supports a practical workaround, not a conclusion that the original generated object was repaired or that a canonical namespace was reset.
